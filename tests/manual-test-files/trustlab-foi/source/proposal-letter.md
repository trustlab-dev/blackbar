Dear Procurement Committee,

TrustLab Inc is pleased to submit this proposal in response to CCSA's
solicitation for fractional Chief Information Officer advisory services
(RFP CCSA-2025-FCIO-014). Our principal, Matt Crossley, would deliver the
engagement directly; this is not a staff-augmentation arrangement. The
combination of MBA-level executive experience, a recent CIO seat at a
post-secondary institution, and current Crown-corporation IT leadership
positions TrustLab uniquely well to address the scope outlined in your
solicitation.

The principal previously held the CIO seat at Camosun College from 2018
to 2019, with full executive accountability for IT strategy, operations,
infrastructure, cybersecurity, and service delivery at a public
post-secondary institution serving 25,000 students and 1,200 staff
across two campuses. In that role he delivered an institution-wide IT
strategy linking academic objectives with infrastructure investment,
established the institution's first cybersecurity programme (endpoint
protection, external penetration testing, security awareness training),
executed the full Ellucian Colleague ERP migration to cloud, modernised
the network across both campuses, and replaced the aging server estate
with hyperconverged infrastructure. That experience represents the
closest analogue in his career to the executive scope described in
CCSA's solicitation.

**On Strategic Leadership and Innovation**, his current work at BC
Transit is centred on exactly the kind of forward-looking digital vision
the RFP describes. He leads the corporation's AI governance framework,
usage policies, and responsible adoption roadmap, balancing innovation
with risk management in a public-sector context. He is also driving the
shift from project-based to product-based delivery, establishing product
ownership disciplines and aligning teams around ongoing value delivery
rather than discrete project cycles. He served as technology lead for
the province-wide Battery Electric Bus programme, integrating fleet
electrification with charge management and operational systems. He is
comfortable acting as the resident futurist the RFP describes, and he
has the credibility from delivery to back it up.

**On Strategic Partnerships and Governance**, the RFP explicitly calls
for establishing and maturing a robust IT governance framework. That is
the work he has done at BC Transit. When he arrived there was no
enterprise PMO, no Architecture Review Board, no formal portfolio
governance, and no structured delivery methodology. He established each
of these over several years: standing up a portfolio prioritisation
process that gave executives transparency into capacity and spend for
the first time, introducing architecture standards and review gates,
founding the Business Relationship Management function to strengthen
cross-departmental engagement, and embedding agile and hybrid delivery
practices.

**On Operational Excellence and Fiscal Stewardship**, he holds
accountability for a $34M CAD technology portfolio at BC Transit, with
business case discipline, KPI reporting, and capacity planning practices
he introduced as part of the portfolio intake process. He works directly
with the VP and Board on portfolio performance, multi-year investment
cycles, and the financial implications of proposed initiatives. He is
comfortable with quarterly forecasting, capital and operating budget
oversight, and the rigour that comes with public funding.

**On Digital Security, Risk and Human Capital**, he provides oversight
of enterprise information risk, service continuity, FOIPPA compliance,
and privacy at BC Transit, and he established the first cybersecurity
programme at Camosun. He has built and mentored a 50-person
multidisciplinary team spanning architecture, analytics, project
delivery, privacy, and information management, and he has served as the
management representative in collective bargaining, informing positions
on artificial intelligence, workforce scheduling, remote work, and
digital tool adoption.

## Engagement model

TrustLab proposes a 0.4 FTE engagement (approximately two days per week),
delivered on-site at CCSA offices one day per fortnight and remotely
otherwise, with a six-month initial term and a mutual option to extend.
A detailed engagement plan, deliverables schedule, and rate card are
attached as Appendix A and Appendix B. References from two prior senior
clients are provided on request and are available to the Procurement
Committee on a confidential basis.

TrustLab would welcome the opportunity to discuss how this proposal can
be refined to fit CCSA's specific context. We are available for an
in-person meeting at your convenience during the week of 6 October 2025.
