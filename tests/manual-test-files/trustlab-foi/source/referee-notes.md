## Referee 1 — Dr. Eleanor Vance

**Role at time of working with the principal:** Vice President,
Education, Camosun College (the principal's direct supervisor 2018-2019)

**Currently:** Retired. Contacted at her personal residence.
**Personal contact details (do not release):** 555-0167; private
residence in Cowichan Valley, BC.

**Date of conversation:** 21 October 2025
**Duration:** 38 minutes
**Format:** Telephone

### Summary of opinions expressed

Dr. Vance described the principal as "the most consequential CIO Camosun
has had in the last decade." Specific points she emphasised:

- Cultural change. "He arrived into an IT organisation that had been
  operating on autopilot for years. Within twelve months he had stood up
  a service-management discipline, dragged the team through a real
  cybersecurity assessment, and got the senior leadership team to take
  IT risk seriously for the first time. That was not a popular project
  internally."

- ERP migration. "The Colleague-to-cloud migration is the work he is
  most known for at Camosun. He delivered it on schedule and on budget,
  which is rare for an ERP migration at our scale."

- Stakeholder management. "He is direct. Some senior peers found him
  too direct in the early months. He course-corrected once he had read
  the room. I would describe him as professionally diplomatic but not
  politically naive — he picks his battles."

- Reason for departure. "He left for a more senior role at a Crown
  corporation. We would have kept him if we could. I have no concerns."

### Verbatim caveats (recorded for completeness)

- On a former direct report (name withheld from this note but available
  on request): "[name] never quite recovered from the change in
  expectations. There was some friction around that. Matt managed it
  well, in my view, but the individual disagreed."

- On a former vendor relationship: "He was firm — possibly too firm —
  with one of our long-standing vendors during a contract dispute. The
  vendor escalated to the President's office. Matt was vindicated on
  the substance but the relationship was strained for the rest of his
  tenure."

### Would you re-hire?

"Without hesitation."

## Referee 2 — Marcus Tan

**Role at time of working with the principal:** Director, Service
Delivery, BC Transit (peer 2019-present)

**Currently:** Same role. Contacted via BC Transit office line.
**Personal contact details (do not release):** Office: 555-0168.
Personal mobile (provided informally): 555-0188.

**Date of conversation:** 22 October 2025
**Duration:** 24 minutes
**Format:** Telephone

### Summary of opinions expressed

Mr. Tan described the principal as "the kind of peer you want on
governance committees because he prepares and he doesn't waste your
time." Specific points:

- Governance build. "He has built what we now consider the IT operating
  model, more or less from scratch. The PMO, the ARB, the portfolio
  intake process — none of those existed before he started. He picked
  fights with finance over portfolio prioritisation and won."

- AI governance. "The AI policy was his initiative. The board level
  conversation about responsible adoption only happened because he
  drafted the framework and brought it forward. I don't know anybody
  else at BC Transit who would have done that."

- Communication style. "Pragmatic. He'll tell you when something is
  not a good idea. He's not going to tell the executive what they want
  to hear. Some find that refreshing, some don't."

- Risk view. "I would say his risk appetite leans conservative on
  cybersecurity and information governance, and more progressive on
  delivery practices. That's a defensible posture for a Crown
  corporation. Probably also a defensible posture for CCSA."

- Bandwidth question. "If you're asking whether he can do this on top
  of his current role — honestly, I don't know. He works hard but his
  current accountability is large. You'd want a clear written
  commitment from him."

### Verbatim caveats (recorded for completeness)

- On a former subordinate: "There was an incident where [name redacted]
  felt micromanaged during the PMO build-out. It went to HR. It was
  resolved without formal action, but it was uncomfortable for several
  months. I mention it for completeness; I do not think it is a
  pattern."

### Would you re-hire?

"In an advisory capacity, yes. As a full-time direct hire, also yes,
but I would want to understand what his next career move looks like
before committing to a multi-year contract."

## Selection Committee summary

Both referees confirm the principal's track record on the substantive
criteria of the RFP (governance build-out, ERP migration leadership,
AI policy authorship, FOIPPA literacy). Both flag the same risk we
identified internally: bandwidth and conflict-of-interest exposure
given the principal's current full-time role at BC Transit. Neither
identifies a substantive concern that would disqualify TrustLab from
short-listing.

Recommendation stands: **shortlist for interview**, with bandwidth and
conflict-of-interest probes built into the interview script.
