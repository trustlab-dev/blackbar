## 1. Summary

CCSA received four proposals in response to RFP CCSA-2025-FCIO-014. This
memo evaluates the proposal from **TrustLab Inc** (principal: Matt
Crossley) against the published criteria and records the Selection
Committee's preliminary view ahead of the 24 October short-list meeting.

Recommendation: **shortlist for interview**, with the qualifications
noted in Section 5.

---

## 2. Proposal in brief

TrustLab Inc is a Victoria-based advisory firm with a single principal
(Matt Crossley). The proposal is for a 0.4 FTE fractional CIO
engagement over an initial six-month term, with the principal personally
delivering the work (no sub-contracting, no staff augmentation). The
proposed day rate is in the upper third of the rate cards received but is
defensible against the principal's stated track record. A copy of the
proposal letter and the principal's CV are attached.

## 4. Selection Committee comments

The following comments were captured at the 15 October 2025 informal
review. They are recorded for the Committee's internal use and should
not be released externally.

- **S. Marquand (Director, Information & Privacy):** "Strongest paper
  candidate of the four. My concern is that the principal is currently
  in a full-time senior role at BC Transit; we need to satisfy ourselves
  that the 0.4 FTE commitment is realistic and that there is no
  conflict-of-interest exposure given some overlap in our supplier base
  with BC Transit's. Recommend probe in interview."

- **D. Patel (Director, Finance):** "Rate is at the top of the range but
  I can defend it if the executive footprint is what's proposed. Would
  want to see one or two named references from CIO-level peers before
  we sign. The fact that he's a Royal Roads MBA alumnus is not a
  selection criterion but it doesn't hurt with our Board."

- **K. Yamashita (Manager, Digital Strategy):** "I've worked indirectly
  with the principal on a paratransit interoperability working group
  through MoTI. He's the real thing on delivery. My only hesitation is
  the same as Susan's: bandwidth. Strongly support short-listing."

## 5. Risks and qualifications

- **Bandwidth risk (high).** The principal's current Director-level
  role at BC Transit is itself a full-time accountability. The 0.4 FTE
  proposed commitment may be optimistic. **Mitigation:** probe in
  interview; consider a written conflict-of-interest declaration as a
  precondition of contract.

- **Conflict-of-interest exposure (medium).** Overlap between CCSA's
  vendor base and BC Transit's, especially in cybersecurity and
  cloud-infrastructure tiers. **Mitigation:** require a recusal protocol
  for any procurement decision involving a shared vendor.

- **Single-principal delivery model (low).** TrustLab is a sole
  practitioner; bus-factor risk if the principal is unavailable. The
  proposal acknowledges this and offers a 30-day exit ramp. Acceptable
  for an advisory engagement of this scope.

## 6. Recommended next steps

1. **Shortlist** TrustLab Inc for interview alongside the second-highest
   scoring proposal (Vendor B — see separate memo).
2. **Reference checks** with two named senior clients prior to the
   interview. The principal has indicated references are available on
   request.
3. **Interview** week of 27 October 2025, in person at CCSA Victoria
   offices. Standard interview panel: S. Marquand, D. Patel,
   K. Yamashita, plus VP Corporate Services as observer.
